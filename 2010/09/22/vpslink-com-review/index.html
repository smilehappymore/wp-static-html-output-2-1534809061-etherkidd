VPSLink Review

Sep 22nd, 2010
Leave a comment | Trackback

This is my review after around 4 months vpslink.com was purchased/acquired by endurance international and was migrated on May 25th, 2010.  To cut it short.  The virtual private server you purchase from vpslink.com will be of near business quality.  I’ll explain below.

The hardware used in Boston for my vps is top notch and I believe them when they say I am on a new server.  Not sure for future customers but they should at least be on servers from 2009/2010 with 16 cores.  Please send a sales inquiry before purchasing on cpu core and server specs on which package you plan to choose.

My server was only down this whole time on September 14, 2010 for 15 minutes for an “emergency maintenance” without prior notification or even a notice after the fact.  One of the benefits of monitoring by 3rd party is that you know this and it’s important because if you are like me and not reboot for months, you really do not know what might not work on a reboot so you need to be notified.  This is not a major complaint because alot of companies do not notify as well which really comes down to good server administration on a vps.  On the bright side it may appear they are actively monitoring the server which after my 48+ downtime with another provider for an hardware issue; it’s somewhat comforting.

My server will periodically lose connection to certain parts of the internet (seriously); usually being unable to route outgoing traffic to some locations but most often still reachable from the internet.  I know this because I have logged ping/trace errors from vpslink but no such errors from my other vps providers to my vpslink server which is the one reason I do not think this is near business quality depending on what your server will be doing.  Typically website hosting is not a problem but if you need a 24/7 ssh connection this will make it a very poor choice to pick as a provider which is not a usual requirement.

The good in my perspective:

  1. VPSLink.com is now owned by reportedly the 3rd largest shared hosting provider? compared to 1and1 and godaddy so they won’t be going anywhere.
  2. Boston datacenter is where their operations are so if there is a hardware problem; I am confident they have the resources required to fix the issue.
  3. The only large provider that offers xen vps

The bad in my perspective:

  1. They are a large shared hosting provider and so you will be dealing with shared hosting support which is usually unimpressive.  This will be a major problem when it comes to routing issues or more technical issues.  For example I had a routing out issue with my vps being able to reach a particular location.  My ticket was held for over 24 hours in tier 2 support (I was told this).  My generic email to [email protected] (their bandwidth provider) confirmed a routing issue 20 hours before a response from vpslink stating if I am still experiencing this issue?!  They did advise to call next time to their 800 number for emergencies…
  2. Their network/routing or  bandwidth provider has continued connection issues(usually outgoing) which is cogent.  The vps I use at enotch also uses cogent in new york and experience similar outgoing issues but at different times.
  3. Currently the price of the vpslink is not very competitive especially for bandwidth
  4. Console access is no longer available  so if you accidentally get firewalled; you need to get a new ip or open a support ticket

The hopeful:

  1. I have noticed their routing for the last 2 weeks of this post in september 22nd is that their routing to vpslink.com has gone through xo and above.net which is good news.  It means there is a greater connectivity to my server but the outgoing is still done through cogentco it seems.

The problematic:

  1. While there were guarantees that the new vpslink.com will follow the same specifications in vps allocations as the old vpslink.com to maintain performance; it is still possible attempts will be made to be oversold.  I have not notice any performance issue to be really concerned though about this point and this does apply to every vps provider and not exclusive to vpslink.

In conclusion I will still remain a customer of vpslink.com because the server hardware is top notch but the network bandwidth is considerably budget category.  They should really triple it to make up for the lack of experienced vps support and their choice of cheaper bandwidth conectivity provider.  The support responses are variable.  I was able to request and complete a reverse dns record in 3 hours but than my recent support request about the routing took over 24 hours.  Not sure how they manage tickets there because it seems it can get lost pretty easily…  They are also the only other xen provider I will use when I require 256MB or less but they are limited to the boston datacenter.   Their website is vpslink.com.
