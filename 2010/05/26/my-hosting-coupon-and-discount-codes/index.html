My Hosting Coupon and Discount Codes

May 26th, 2010
Leave a comment

Due to the recent sale of my vps provider; I think it is a very good idea to diversify my vps accounts for situations where the vps hosting provider you have is going through technical problems which affects your business.  This post is dedicated to hosting coupons, discount codes, refferal codes to providers I either am hosting at or consider hosting at.  Currently there are 3 on my list.

I want to clarify a few things from my over 10 years of experience and this is how I see the hosting world.  The first thing you have to remember is most hosting providers will usually have the same hardware (typically dell or supermicro servers) with a variety of cpu/ram types.  The second thing is that I like paying in a year and not monthly (this is why I perfer large providers with several years in the business who are registered companies in the united states because there are plenty of hosting providers disappear very quickly).  When problems occur it usually comes down to 3 things.  The hardware (most often the harddrive  fails) which will require a fsck disk which can take a long time depending on how large the drive is.  The second often is power failures which would affect hardware like drives.  The third is network issues from routers to bandwidth providers (cut in the fiber link) which can be hard to diagnose and out of the control of your hosting provider and even the datacenter.  A larger provider who maybe owns the datacenter or colocate with an office will be best to handle issues you will most likely face but because they are larger they will have larger costs like a billing department, properly paid employees which is acceptable.  Nothing wrong with small providers but if your hosting provider consists of one or two person; they may provide a more personalized experience but will often be powerless to really help when their servers (if they even own them) have problems.  Some have good experiences with small providers but if you rely on your website to be on 24/7 than I firmly believe you should stick with large providers with the capital to provide what they advertise and deal with server issues.

There are several types of hosting:

  • shared hosting
  • reseller hosting (similar to shared hosting but you can resell)
  • virtual private server
  • dedicated server

When you start out it will usually be shared hosting.  The main drawback with shared hosting is usually the inability to control mysql configuration which has a huge effect on performance in several content management systems.  If you simply using a basic html/php site than you should stick with shared hosting.  Shared Hosting are usually hosted on one LARGE powerful server so it can handle a very substantial website till in most situations the database can no longer substain it in my experience.  When I had drupal it was never fast in a shared hosting provider and performance got worst with more domains that had different content management systems on it because of the database issue.  I currently recommend fatcow.com because they are the same company that bought up spry.com/vpslink.com to provide virtual private servers.  You can see see uptime at http://linkuser.fatcow.com/ and determine if it suit your needs.  Most shared hosting providers will have no problem with a very busy wordpress site but make sure to set a 404 error page.  You want to set this because alot of providers will redirect your page not found errors to their ads.  This even happens with my verizon dsl provider(via their dns server which redirects wrong web sites) or even browsers but some people get really irrational upset when they find out error pages go to their providers ads that can sometimes be objectionable.

Reseller hosting is best when you need more power than a shared hosting provider can give you with added benefits of selling.  This is a great choice when you believe in the provider you are reselling while making some money in the process.  This gives you the added bonus of possibly setting up your own 404 error redirects which your clients will be redirected to if they didn’t set their own error pages.  Very devious indeed.  I currently use ezpzhosting.co.uk with their server in atlanta to resell hosting to customers who may not be reliable enough to keep their passwords safe, use safe computers or wireless which may compromise the server impacting my clients.

VPS hosting is a great choice when you have a fairly large site and you want greater control that shared/reseller hosting cannot provide.  For example you can install a virtual private network (VPN) and use that to secure your wireless connection in public areas. VPN’s are generally encrypted like SSL connections banks use but you just have to make sure you are connected with the appropriate client/gui on your computer.  You can setup a asterisk VOIP server and many other fun things which is not possible for a shared/reseller hosting account.  A VPS usually includes a free unique ip that’s not the same as a shared/reseller hosting provider which allows better and safer outgoing mail.  The only negative is that datacenters which your server is located and your vps hosting provider have a few strict rules about email spam AND security though you may find shared hosting terms of service to be more complicated and longwinded; a vps can do much more damage on the internet.  Also remember that Since I paid yearly I am very proactive in my security to prevent a forefeit of my annual payments from someone using my server to send out thousands of spam email or as a server engaging in Denial of Service Attacks.  This requires a greater deal of technical knowledge and attention but affords you flexibility, power, and control.  Performance is best when you have a large memory to give to database and tweak it to improve content management systems.  I highly recommend linode.com for a first vps with a WHOOPING 512MB ram which is more than enough for any site(s) with a very impressive control panel.  If you find you do not need 512mb or less I currently use vpslink to host business sites.  I formerly use enotch vps for backup and development sites because of their use of openvz (aka virtuozzo, parallels) which is a less isolated virtualization technology with a few shortcomings that I would NOT recommend if you need reliability or make money from your sites.  This website is dedicated to managing virtual private servers so feel free to read all the articles specifically the guides which go through what you need to do on a vps.

Dedicated Servers will not be covered much because basically what you can do with a vps with xen virtualization you can apply it to a dedicated server.  The only negative is it usually does not allow you to easily change operating systems like what’s available at a vps control panel with your choice of ubuntu, centos, debian, etc. operating systems.  The positive thing about owning a dedicated server is that you are no longer sharing with other customers with cpu/ram/hard drive.  This usually means better performance even though the server itself may not be as powerful as what’s powering a shared/reseller or vps server.
