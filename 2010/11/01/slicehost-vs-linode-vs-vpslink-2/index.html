Slicehost vs Linode vs Vpslink 2

Nov 1st, 2010
Leave a comment | Trackback

Things changed alot since my first review.  Slicehost started offering 32bit operating systems and many different slices.  Vpslink transferred hands.  Linode changed the entire playing field by offering linode 512mb upgrades to their existing 360mb plans and is now the lowest available plan of theirs.   Each still provide a good choice for what what you are looking for:

Slicehost

  • lowest offering is 256mb xen based vps
  • subsidiary of rackspace.com
  • monthly billing
  • Xen only
  • 4 quads (cpu)
  • 1-800 number contact

Linode

  • lowest offering is 512mb xen based vps
  • Best price offering starting with 4 quads with an additional discount for annual subscriptions
  • 4 quads (cpu)
  • Independent company dedicated to vps
  • Refunds only by request of cancellation minus 5 dollar fee

Vpslink

  • Lowest offering is 64mb xen and openvz vps
  • Largest monthly bandwidth offering
  • Subsidiary of Endurance International a large shared hosting provider.
  • 24/7 800 number
  • Extra IPs free with Link 4 and above

In a nutshell:

Linode.com is the first choice if you are a beginner to experienced administrator.  The ram and very quick support ticket responses are makes them the best available vps provider in the planet.  I make this claim not lightly or without evidence.  The only negative is that it’s always possible they will be sold and that would be a problem that their refund policy for annual payments is you lose the discount and a 5 dollar fee which makes paying annually/biannually less beneficial at second glance.

Slicehost.com is the best choice for business hosting due to their pricing and quick response times.  They are owned by a datacenter and I do not see them selling it off anytime soon.  Their datacenters are located in middle america which may not be best for foreign visitors but a plus if you are an american business.  Their best plan now is the $25 for 384MB and I think the higher price makes them less likely to experience abusive users you will typically find from budget hosts and fraud from foreign customers.  Their annual payment includes a bonus 10 percent added to the account but there are no refunds.

Vpslink.com is the best choice for experienced administrators who would not require their slower support ticket response times since their acquisition from Endurance International however they do have a very reachable 24/7 toll free telephone line.  Hopefully this will change because their customer support is in phoenix when their datacenter is in Boston.  I think much will be improved if they can separate support tickets of their vps clients to tier 2 in the Boston datacenter; however I have not heard of much downtime or at all since their migration was completed.  I highly recommend them if you require 256mb and lower (xen) because they are the cheapest and use enterprise hardware.  They are only located in one datacenter and use cheaper bandwidth provider cogent… I would not recommend placing more than one DNS server there due to network issues that will occasionally happen to any datacenter but is worst when you have limited bandwidth providers.  My full review is listed at this link with some graphs.
