Thinking of VPS VDS – First 3 Steps

Aug 7th, 2009
Leave a comment | Trackback

You have reached this page because:

1) You have tried free hosting.

2) You have tried paid hosting.

3) You are thinking about hosting.

I think that covers it.  Problem with many shared hosting environments and almost all free hosting environments are the restrictions on php functions.  These functions affect how your CMS (Content Management System) interacts with other services.  For example you use google apps for your domain email and would like to use a gmail email account to send out mail.  Imagine your surprise when your PAID hosting environment restricts your fopen (also known as fsock, fsocket) only to port 80.  What that means is that you can’t use gmail in your favorite CMS like wordpress, joomla, drupal, etc. because you cannot establish a smtp/imap connection to your free email provider.  There are many other examples of these restrictions because of security precautions taken for other customers.

Welcome to VPS (Virtual Private Server) or VDS (Virtual Dedicated Server) where you have unrestricted access to your own partition of a physical server.  The only limitation is your RAM, Hard drive space, and of course the CPU load of everything you want to run.  Think of it as your own computer at a Hosting Facility versus you borrowing a computer in the public library.  Which would you perfer?

Now here comes the first steps.

1) Be serious about your website.

Many people get the mistake of creating websites when they are not really serious about it.  This means you really need to know what programs is powering everything you need in a basic website and how to configure it.  When you get shared hosting you simply need to know some html and uploading the files over.  Some people skip this step and install user friendly packaged computer applications like forums, blogs, or flexible content management systems.  This is fine in most cases till you want to extend those applications beyond the installation steps which most people will want to do; otherwise you can just stick with blogger.com.  You have to learn and the best way is using a search engine.  If you can’t get a general idea of what to search for or where to move on to next…  it’s going to be a problem and seeking professional help is always something to consider.

2) It takes time

At this point in time you have to be aware of how you learn best.  Are you the visual type?  Do you have to work on it for you to learn something?  Can you learn by reading a book?  Everything takes time but depending on how you learn best you can take the plunge now to VPS (most hosting providers provide 30 days trial) or you can practice on your own computer using virtualbox and a Debian/CENTOS image.  Practice, read, but be patient.

3) Consider your needs

I generally do not recommend a VPS that has less than 256MB Ram as your first trial.  You should spend your trial with the best chance of success.  Even though it is tempting to choose the lowest available VPS that is generally 64MB Ram it is a horrible mistake.  Consider your own computer at home; would you want to work on your computer with 64MB Ram?  It should not take more than 3 weeks for you to setup your VPS to host a website with the available guides here or online otherwise to determine your RAM needs.  At that point you can decide for yourself to change plans.  Some VPS provide 64MB with additional BURST memory which may work but it’s always better to overestimate your needs than underestimate for the first few months.  You don’t want your visitors to get frustrated from slowness/downtime from your overloaded server.

These first 3 steps is really all you need to start with a VPS or life in general.  It’s great advice you have probably heard before so listen to it and apply it.
