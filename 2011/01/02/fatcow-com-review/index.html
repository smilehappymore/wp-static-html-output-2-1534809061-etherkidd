Fatcow.com Review

Jan 2nd, 2011
Leave a comment | Trackback

Here is my review for fatcow hosting located at fatcow.com

An important thing to note with this review is that shared hosting is in general more powerful than a virtual private server (vps) that is less than 256mb ram and easier to administer.  However power does not equate to speed and versatility.  From experience; depending on what you are hosting with a quality vps provider that does not oversell resources; uptime and performance will be superior than a shared hosting account.  This includes so called grid-service like mediatemple which had trouble handling a drupal installation with no users due to database performance.   However there are times a shared hosting account will be a better choice if you keep several things in mind.

1) Is the website I will be hosting be database intensive?

This can mean several things.  The problem with one size meets all on shared hosting is that there will be database restrictions.  The most undisclosed is the mysql max_connections set by the server.  This usually is low which is around 10-15 but rarely more than 20.  The reason usually is that each connection(also known as threads) will cost a certain amount of memory.  Under a default mysql install with no configuration; it would be around 4M(shared hosting probably tuned this down to 2M) per connection so in theory every person on the server will be reserved at least 20M of memory if they use 10 connections to the database.  This does not include the cpu/data/memory with running a query.  A typical wordpress blog out the box is around 20 per page visit.  When people install software on a shared hosting account like various intensive content management systems (cms) it can quickly be a serious problem.  It does not help when they provide instant one click installs of such software.   A few providers also set a database size limit to their unlimited database offers so you need to know your requirements before hand if it’s specific.

2) Will the website have many visits or file hits?

This is also a very undisclosed variable that a lot of shared hosting providers set which is relatively high it poses an issue because 1) will restrict you on what type of database script you can run while this 2) issue will restrict how popular your site is even if you create a website that doesn’t rely on a database.

3) Will I be making money on this website with shopping software?

Database limitations will affect you greatly in this retrospect especially if you have a large inventory that requires storing and retrieving that data; plus most likely external connections to payment processors which the server firewall and security policy can cause problems with.

With this in mind, shared hosting is best for medium to low traffic niche sites that need good bandwidth with rich media like a local music band, a theater group, or portfolio site.  Any cms/script/forum (look for one which uses few queries).  I personally use wordpress and mybboard however even though they are light weight…. you will typically experience problems at around 100 simultaneous users on a forum or too many third party plugins in wordpress.

Fatcow.com while unlimited usually means “normal use” as is the policy of almost all shared hosting providers.   The soft limit I would imagine is 10gb hard space and 5gb bandwidth a day (200gb a month) with a daily hit rate of 75,000 visits a day with around 50,000 database queries an hour.  These limits are rarely published and generally hard to obtain however you should be aware of these soft limits in shared hosting so your website is not suspended which is a frequent complaint around the internet.  Hopefully from reading my soft limits you can understand that these numbers are actually pretty good for low traffic sites which most internet sites are and provided it is not overly sold (most are though) a stricter limit may actually help you maintain a stable website performance.  Keep in mind it wouldn’t help you if you have a 100 users taking 20 database queries a page visit to someone with a 100 users requiring 600 database queries a page visit.

The reason I recommend fatcow is because they are located in east coast at Boston, Massachusetts which provides good connectivity to North America/Europe.  They are powered by 100% wind energy and also a subsidiary of Endurance International which owns a great deal of hosting labels including powweb.  They have a 800 number which you can contact 24/7 and the uptime is very decent.  You can check out http://linkuser.fatcow.com which has 100% uptime at 30 minute intervals which is not too reliable but at least you know if is up and fatcow is an established company that won’t go anywhere.  The price is ok because I know the company is not going anywhere and it fits my low end hosting needs without worrying about bandwidth problems.  Generally I only use it when I need a customized layout and don’t want to use video sharing sites like vimeo and youtube.  Podcasts are a bad idea on shared hosting and you should be weary of it.  This is not a particular glowing review because you really need to know what limits are if you want a stable hosting experience.  You can probably find much more lenient hosting terms from other hosting providers and I encourage you to ask close friends with similar websites with similar content/popularity (keep an eye on what database usage they have) but I personally perfer stricter terms and limits because you really can’t expect to pay around $10 a month and it act like a dedicated server.

I intentionally did not go into the package and control panel details since realistically… most users will not use them and it’s more reliable using the established email services like yahoo/msn/gmail (just forward them in the control panel).  The only real benefit which doesn’t seem to be advertised is the akamai which will speed up your text content views (which is any request be it just css or html and be 200k or less in size) for the first 100,000 file accesses per month which resets every month; however if your html page includes separate css/javascript files it will be quickly used up since the separate file includes count toward your limit.  In this situation I would be cautious of using a monitoring service that accesses text content because I monitor my site per 60-90 seconds over a few locations; that is around 100,000 hits on alone per month.  The control panel is overly basic which could be a good thing but a few actions are awkward and difficult to find but none which really affect your web hosting as you should rely on ftp once you activate your shared hosting.  Unfortunately fatcow.com shared hosting is heavily marketed which makes reliability challenging from new users who really wants unlimited to mean they can run a youtube site.

The general rules are that if you are making more than 5 times what you are spending on hosting,  you should consider alternatives on keeping your website up like a content delivery networks (CDN) or some sort fail-over hosting.  Shared hosting is generally best for local promotional purposes;  just be sure you have a clear backup but there are many less polished free alternatives like facebook, wordpress, youtube, vimeo, blogspot, etc. that is equally adequate(better performance) but less professional.  Any cloud type service is generally gimmicky, cost more, and not the best solution for database issues.  Most successful sites will have dedicated servers first and cloud services as backup.  I plan on discontinuing any share hosting solution because I found a high bandwidth provider for my media needs but that may not be a viable option for most users starting out with my bandwidth needs. Their plan is annual in term and have a 35 dollar early termination fee and the option of charging for the remainder(maybe I am reading it wrong).  They do have a 30 day unconditional guarantee.  Things change so look at their policy if you decide to sign up.  They are located at fatcow.com.  I have no problem with them but I know how much resources I am using.
